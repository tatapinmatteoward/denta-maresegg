### What Are Impacted Wisdom Teeth?

Impacted wisdom teeth occur when there is insufficient space in the jaw for the molars to erupt properly. As a result, they become trapped beneath the gum line or within the jawbone. This condition can lead to a range of issues, including:

- **Pain and Swelling**: Pressure from the impacted tooth can cause intense pain and swelling in the jaw and surrounding tissues.

- **Infection (Pericoronitis)**: Partially erupted teeth create pockets where bacteria can accumulate, leading to infection, bad breath, and difficulty opening the mouth.

- **Damage to Adjacent Teeth**: The impacted tooth may exert pressure on neighboring molars, causing damage or misalignment.

- **Cysts or Tumors**: In rare but serious cases, fluid-filled sacs or tumors may develop around the impacted tooth, potentially damaging bone and surrounding structures.
